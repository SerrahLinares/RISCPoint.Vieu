What Is Vieu

Vieu is not a lead generation tool. It is an intelligence layer built on 9 billion executive relationships that tells your team which accounts to pursue, why now, who to talk to, how to reach them through your existing network, and what to say when you get there. The output is structured to be immediately actionable by your sales team without additional research.

What the Graph Indexes
People, Companies, and the Signals Between Them
Vieu pulls from 40+ public data sources and enriches across three layers:
  • + Professional history and current roles
  • + Social media interactions: shared posts, comments, likes
  • + Conference panels and speaking appearances together
  • + Alumni and shared company tenure
  • + News mentions, press coverage, and public announcements
  • + Job postings, technographic signals, and company filings
Relationship Scoring
Strength, Not Just Existence
Every connection in the graph is scored for strength based on frequency, recency, and type of interaction. A path where two people worked together for two years and have interacted publicly multiple times ranks higher than a one-time connection. The strongest paths surface first.
Applied to GTM
From Signal to Meeting
Given any target account and any target stakeholder, Vieu finds the shortest, strongest path from your network to that person — with the context to make the introduction credible: what connects the two people, when they last interacted, and what to reference.

GTM Priorities: First 4 Months

The goal is 10 ACE registrations and a repeatable warm-intro pipeline across RISCPoint's three growth vectors: commercial advisory, federal 3PAO, and RADAR PTaaS. Every priority below is sequenced to move that outcome forward.

  • Build your platform workflow in the first two weeks — speed to first warm path matters
    The biggest predictor of outcome in a Tier 3 engagement is how fast the team moves from onboarding to active outreach. The goal is a warm path actioned within the first week. Vieu CS will walk the growth team through the platform, agree on the priority account list, and confirm the ICP filter set so the first searches return immediately actionable results. The 1,000-lead target is a floor, not a ceiling — the faster the team builds the workflow, the faster it compounds.
  • Lead with trust erosion as the opening thesis — RISCPoint's own market narrative is the entry point
    Jake Nix's April 2026 thought leadership post — that commodity SOC 2 programs are creating hidden risk and that differentiated firms win through credibility — is the most powerful cold-to-warm conversion tool in the market right now. Every outreach from Vieu-sourced warm paths should anchor on this narrative: the person receiving the introduction already lives in this market and recognizes the problem. Vieu identifies the accounts and stakeholders most likely to agree with RISCPoint's point of view.
  • Use technographics to target Vanta and Drata accounts approaching the advisory ceiling
    Organizations using automated compliance platforms often hit a point where the tool alone cannot carry them through a rigorous audit, a FedRAMP authorization, or a complex CMMC engagement. These accounts are actively looking for an advisory partner — they just do not always know it yet. Vieu's technographic layer surfaces them before they issue an RFP. For RISCPoint, this is the highest-conversion commercial entry point because the need is already implicit in the tech stack.
  • Activate the AWS partner network for co-sell into cloud security and FedRAMP accounts
    RISCPoint's AWS Advanced Tier and Global Security and Compliance Acceleration Partner status create co-sell infrastructure that is currently underleveraged for new logo acquisition. Vieu maps AWS partner account managers and ecosystem contacts who work with both RISCPoint and their target accounts, surfacing the warm paths that turn AWS co-sell from a passive benefit into an active pipeline source. ACE registrations should flow directly from these conversations.
  • Run RADAR as a parallel track — the buyer persona is different and the motion is faster
    RADAR's AI-powered PTaaS product has a distinct buyer: CISO and VP Security at mid-market SaaS and cloud companies. This is a more transactional conversation than advisory compliance work, and it often opens the door to broader RISCPoint engagements. Vieu builds a separate warm-path target list for RADAR's buyer persona so both tracks run simultaneously without competing for the same outreach capacity.
  • Target FedRAMP 20x and CMMC deadline pressure as the highest-urgency federal entry points
    FedRAMP 20x Phase 1 pilot ran through September 2025; broader requirements are formalizing now. CSPs navigating the new program need a trusted 3PAO with current program knowledge. CMMC Level 2/3 certification deadlines are creating concentrated spend among defense contractors in 2026. Both motions reward early trusted access over volume. Vieu surfaces warm paths to FedRAMP program managers, authorization leads, and CMMC program owners at the highest-priority targets.
